Abstract

This paper proposes a novel approach that integrates depth estimation from monocular images with Virtual Holonomic Constraints (VHC) based control to enhance bipedal robot locomotion. Utilizing deep learning for depth estimation, our method transforms monocular image data into actionable 3D information, crucial for navigating obstacles and stairs. The integration of perception and control is simulated using NVIDIA Isaac Sim, where synthetic data from a simulated environment informs the robot control system. Successful simulations will be followed by real-world testing to validate the system's performance.
